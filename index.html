Who is Doctor Sawyer?

A medical diagnostic assistant.

Doctors and nurses are busy, and hospital wait times are long, so what about bringing in some robots for help?

We introduce Sawyer the robot to perform a few tasks that doctors and nurses do on a daily basis: temperature measuring, pulse sensing, and tumor detection. But wait… how does the patient interact with doctor Sawyer?

This is the plan: the patient is called from the waiting room, they lie on a table, and Dr. Sawyer measures their vitals, determines if there is a cause for concern, and relays this to the physician. Dr. Sawyer should be modular, so that we can add and edit his functionalities at any time, and when he is free, he should move to a “standby” position ready for the next task. Dr. Sawyer should be friendly, and not hurt the patient in any way. We want him to improve patient satisfaction!

In the real world, Dr. Sawyer can be used for a variety of purposes:

  • as a personal medical assistant to keep track of your vitals
  • as a doctor’s assistant to lessen physicians’ and nurses’ workloads and to improve efficiency in a hospital
  • as a rescue robot that can find survivors in the aftermath of a natural disaster and determine their risk level
